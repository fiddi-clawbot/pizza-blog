## About Mas Pizza

Mas Pizza occupies the sweet spot between tradition and innovation. Located in the atmospheric Born neighborhood, near the Gothic Quarter, this intimate 35-seat restaurant proves you can honor Italian pizza tradition while exploring new flavor combinations.

### What Makes It Special

- **Quality Ingredients**: Italian imports (prosciutto, cheese, tomatoes) + Spanish produce (seasonal vegetables, local greens)
- **Thoughtful Combinations**: Creative toppings that complement rather than compete
- **Technique**: Wood-fired oven, 48-hour dough fermentation, precise timing
- **Plating Philosophy**: Pizza as a composed dish—balance, color, texture
- **Neighborhood Location**: Central location but feels hidden, intimate

### Signature Offerings

- **Burrata y Prosciutto** - Fresh burrata, prosciutto di Parma, arugula, lemon. Elegance on crust.
- **Pesto di Pistacchio** - Sicilian pistachio pesto, fior di latte, sun-dried tomato
- **Rapa y Anchoa** - Turnip greens, white anchovies, garlic, chili
- **Gorgonzola y Mela** - Gorgonzola, thin apple slices, walnuts, honey drizzle

### Ambiance

Modern-meets-rustic. Exposed stone walls, soft lighting, open kitchen. Contemporary art on walls. Welcoming but refined. Background music leans jazz and contemporary Italian. 25-35 age demographic, educated palate, not pretentious.

### Pricing

- Pizzas: €14-20
- Sides (cured meats, cheese boards): €8-15
- Desserts: €6-9
- **Average per person**: €16-24 (food, no drinks)

### Verdict

Mas Pizza is for curious eaters who want pizza innovation but won't tolerate gimmicks. Anna and Marco's approach—master tradition, then evolve—is a model more pizzerias should follow.

**Best for**: Adventurous pizza lovers, date nights, foodie explorers, anyone wanting to experience modern Italian pizza done right.
