## About Berberena

Berberena is pizza as cultural bridge. Dimitri's philosophy: pizza is Italian, but the Mediterranean is everyone's. His pizzas pay homage to Italy while drawing inspiration from the broader region—Greek feta, Spanish peppers, Moroccan spices, Turkish influences.

### What Makes It Special

- **Mediterranean Perspective**: Pizza tradition anchored, ingredients borrowed from entire region
- **Flavor Balance**: Bold toppings that work together, not compete
- **Sourcing**: Collaboration with Greek, Spanish, and local producers
- **Open-minded Spirit**: Respects Italian tradition but refuses to be limited by it
- **Located in Born**: Walkable from Gothic Quarter, in a lively neighborhood

### Signature Offerings

- **Berberena Blend** - Feta, roasted peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, pine nuts, Greek yogurt, olive tapenade
- **Athenian** - Feta, olives, tomatoes, oregano, phyllo crust base
- **Española** - Chorizo, piquillo peppers, manchego cheese, caramelized onions
- **Moroccan Spice** - Harissa-spiced tomato base, lamb sausage, cilantro, pomegranate

### Ambiance

Eclectic, warm, welcoming. Mediterranean colors (warm ochre, deep blue accents), comfortable seating, music rotating between Greek, Spanish, and Italian classics. 30-40 seat dining room, full bar. Vibe is friendly, unpretentious, neighborhood-focused.

### Pricing

- Pizzas: €13-19
- Mediterranean plates: €8-14
- Desserts: €5-8
- **Average per person**: €15-22

### Verdict

Berberena works because Dimitri respects pizza fundamentals while confidently blending the wider Mediterranean. The result feels less like fusion (often a code word for confused) and more like authentic cultural exchange. A joy.

**Best for**: Mediterranean food lovers, adventurous eaters, those wanting something different but still genuine, cultural explorers.
